Cavite Escapes

A Journey Through the Heart of Philippine Heritage

The Heritage Province

Known as the "Historical Capital of the Philippines," Cavite stands as a testament to the nation's bravery and growth. Located at the southern tip of Manila Bay, it is a province where revolutionary landmarks meet modern urban developments. From its cool highlands to its historical shrines, Cavite offers travelers a diverse range of experiences—whether you are looking for a historical pilgrimage, a mountain hike, or a world-class culinary adventure.

Featured Destinations

I. Tagaytay City: The Highland Sanctuary

Tagaytay is Cavite's crown jewel. Famous for its perpetual cool breeze and the stunning view of Taal Volcano, it is the perfect retreat for leisure. Visitors can enjoy the famous Bulalo, visit the People's Park in the Sky, or simply enjoy the majestic scenery from the ridge.

II. Kawit: The Cradle of Independence

Kawit holds the echoes of the past within the walls of the Aguinaldo Shrine. It was on the balcony of this mansion that General Emilio Aguinaldo proclaimed Philippine independence in 1898. The architecture and secret rooms of the house offer a deep dive into the country's revolutionary history.

III. Maragondon: The Wilderness Frontier

Maragondon is a destination for nature lovers and adventurers. It is home to the famous Mt. Pico de Loro, known for its "Parrot's Beak" summit. The town also features the historic Bonifacio Trial House, where the revolutionary leader Andres Bonifacio was tried.

IV. Silang: The Agricultural Gem

Silang is known for its rolling hills and fertile soil, making it the hub for organic farms and coffee plantations. Destinations like Gourmet Farms and various botanical gardens offer a "farm-to-table" experience that celebrates the province's agricultural wealth.

About Cavite

Geography: Cavite is bounded by Manila Bay to the north, Laguna to the east, and Batangas to the south. Its diverse terrain includes coastal areas and high ridges.

History: The province played a central role in the Philippine Revolution against Spanish rule, earning its title as the Historical Capital.

Economy: Beyond tourism, Cavite is a major industrial hub, home to several economic zones and manufacturing plants, while maintaining its strong roots in agriculture and fishing.
